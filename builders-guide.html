Welcome to TrekCraft

Welcome to TrekCraft's Builder's Guide! This document will help you understand our building standards, approach, and what we look for in new team members. Whether you're considering applying or just curious about how we work, this guide covers everything you need to know.

What is TrekCraft? We're the world's premiere sci-fi themed creative megabuilding server in Minecraft. Our build team works together to bring larger-than-life creations right to your screen by fully realizing ships like the Enterprise-D and Millennium Falcon. We're committed to constantly raising the bar and setting the standard as the best and most inclusive community of our kind.

Our Approach

We're not just building ships — we're creating a living Star Trek universe. TrekCraft operates like a union: we all contribute to group projects, make textures, help new builders, and in exchange, we all benefit from the community resources.

What Makes Us Different

  • Lore Accuracy: Every build respects Star Trek canon and design language
  • Functional Design: Our ships aren't just pretty — they're designed to work
  • Community Focus: We build together, learn together, and grow together
  • Long-term Vision: Projects can take months or years to complete
  • Forward Thinking: We look at modern examples, not builds from 10 years ago
  • Individual Expression: Make builds your own with your own spin, but keep them cohesive

Our Values

  • A safe space for LGBTQIA2+ folk, free of harassment, bullying, and hate-speech
  • A home for like-minded folks who enjoy pushing the limits of Minecraft
  • A communal server focused on group projects with personal interest breaks
  • A Discord promoting healthy constructive discussion and inclusivity
What TrekCraft Is NOT
  • A place for negative, hateful, or bigoted people
  • A place for toxic, overly political, or hateful conversations
  • Your private server or personal storage
  • A bubble-wrapped community — things can sting, but they should never burn

What We Build

Every builder at TrekCraft does their best to learn, improve, and push the boundaries of what is possible at the scale of Minecraft. We should never be looking at what we did before as a guide, but as a lesson in what worked and what didn't. We use modern examples to indicate what is appropriate.

Scale & Proportions

  • Corridor Widths: 3 and 4 blocks, with roofs between 3-5 blocks above
  • Deck Height: Ship decks should aim for 4 blocks tall per deck (3 or 5 is fine for alignment)
  • Room Sizing: Functional spaces that feel lived-in
  • Ship Proportions: Realistic length-to-width ratios

Interior Design

  • Consistent Styling: Each faction has distinct design language
  • Functional Layout: Rooms connect logically with clear flow
  • Greebling: Technical details that add realism
  • Lighting: Strategic use of light sources for atmosphere
Exterior Design
  • Hull Shape: Smooth, aerodynamic curves where appropriate
  • Nacelle Design: Proper warp engine styling
  • Surface Detail: Panels, windows, and structural elements
  • Color Schemes: Faction-appropriate color palettes

What We Enforce

  • Planning Blocks: Always used for any project
  • Dead Space: Should be filled to minimize block faces
  • Hull-Cleaning: Proper deck planning should precede build out
  • Hero-Builds: Should be accurate to what the audience knows
  • Lag Prevention: Limitations on lag-heavy blocks
  • Signage: Limited to hero areas or for planning

What We DON'T Enforce

  • Planning Techniques: You can set your own methods and pacing
  • Original Builds: Non-canon builds have additional freedoms
  • Exact Methods: We ask for same-era "feels the same" but you can put your own spin
  • Re-using Components: Welcomed if the original builder is okay with it

Tools We Use

We use a comprehensive custom resource pack with thousands of Star Trek-themed blocks and textures. Key categories include:

Interior Blocks

  • Wall Panels: LCARS-style wall coverings in multiple colors
  • Floor Tiles: Federation, Klingon, and Romulan flooring
  • Ceiling Panels: Integrated lighting and ventilation
  • Doors: Automatic sliding doors and bulkheads

Technical Elements

  • Computer Consoles: Bridge stations and workstations
  • Engineering Equipment: Warp cores, power conduits, control panels
  • Medical Equipment: Biobeds, scanners, and treatment stations
  • Security Systems: Force fields, weapons lockers, security stations
Furniture & Decor
  • Beds & Quarters: Crew accommodations and VIP suites
  • Dining Areas: Replicators, tables, and lounge furniture
  • Recreation: Holodecks, gym equipment, and relaxation areas
  • Storage: Cargo containers, lockers, and supply cabinets

Design Styles

Understanding Star Trek aesthetics is crucial for creating authentic builds. Each faction has distinct design principles:

Federation (Starfleet)

  • Colors: Blues, grays, and whites with gold accents
  • Style: Clean, functional, and optimistic
  • Materials: Smooth panels, integrated lighting, ergonomic design
  • Layout: Open spaces, clear sightlines, accessibility focus

Klingon Empire

  • Colors: Dark grays, blacks, and reds
  • Style: Angular, aggressive, and utilitarian
  • Materials: Rough textures, exposed structural elements
  • Layout: Compact, efficient, combat-oriented
Romulan Star Empire
  • Colors: Greens, grays, and dark tones
  • Style: Elegant curves, asymmetry, and mystery
  • Materials: Smooth surfaces, hidden technology, sophisticated design
  • Layout: Complex, layered spaces with strategic positioning

Ship Basics

Every ship needs certain key areas to function properly. Understanding these requirements helps create realistic, functional designs:

Command & Control

  • Bridge: Command center with stations for all departments
  • Ready Room: Captain's private office and conference space
  • Conference Room: Meeting space for senior staff
  • Security Office: Tactical operations and security monitoring

Engineering & Operations

  • Main Engineering: Warp core, power systems, and engineering stations
  • Computer Core: Central computer systems and data storage
  • Environmental Systems: Life support, air circulation, and waste management
  • Transporter Rooms: Personnel and cargo transport facilities

Crew Support

  • Medical Bay: Sickbay, treatment areas, and medical labs
  • Crew Quarters: Living accommodations for all personnel
  • Mess Hall: Dining areas and food preparation
  • Recreation: Holodecks, gym, and relaxation areas

Mission Support

  • Shuttlebay: Small craft storage and maintenance
  • Science Labs: Research facilities and experimentation areas
  • Cargo Bays: Storage for supplies and equipment
  • Armory: Weapons storage and security equipment

Building Process

Consider this checklist not a bible, but a guide for deciding whether a project is a good fit for TrekCraft:

Project Requirements
  • Multiple Builders: Do you have builders committed (not just interested) to help?
  • Reference Material: Do we have enough reference material or new ideas to build the entire ship?
  • Build Lead: Does the build lead feel capable and comfortable to run this project for 2-5 years?
  • Uniqueness: Is this build a copy of something we already have? If so, what makes it special?
  • Skill Mix: Does the build team have the right mix of skills (deck planning, hull work, interior design, system design, etc.)?
  • Resources: Do we have the blocks you need? Will you need textures or custom assets?

Planning Phase

  • Research: Study canon references and design precedents
  • Concept Development: Create sketches and deck plans
  • Team Discussion: Share ideas and get feedback from the team
  • Resource Planning: Determine what blocks and materials we'll need

Construction Phase

  • Framework First: Build the basic hull shape and deck structure
  • Interior Layout: Plan and build room arrangements
  • Detailed Work: Add furniture, equipment, and decorative elements
  • Polish & Review: Refine details and get team feedback
Collaboration
  • Regular Check-ins: Share progress and get input
  • Team Reviews: Get feedback on design choices and execution
  • Knowledge Sharing: Learn from each other's techniques and approaches
  • Mentorship: Experienced builders help guide newer team members

Quality Matters

Being on TrekCraft means being open to feedback from others whether it be positive or constructive. Choosing to build on TrekCraft means agreeing to be praised or questioned on the things you're doing. Feedback is the ultimate gift, and to decline it is to essentially say "I don't care."

Technical Excellence

  • Clean Construction: Smooth surfaces, proper alignment, no gaps
  • Consistent Scale: Proper proportions throughout the build
  • Thoughtful Detailing: Meaningful greebling and decorative elements
  • Functional Layout: Spaces that make sense for their intended use

Creative Vision

  • Original Ideas: Fresh approaches to familiar concepts
  • Problem Solving: Clever solutions to design challenges
  • Attention to Detail: Small touches that bring builds to life
  • Cohesive Style: Consistent design language throughout

Team Compatibility

  • Communication: Clear, respectful, and constructive
  • Collaboration: Willing to work with others and share ideas
  • Growth Mindset: Open to feedback and continuous improvement
  • Commitment: Dedicated to long-term project success

Feedback Guidelines

  • Constructive Criticism: Criticism should be constructive — we're all S-tier players helping each other improve
  • Honest Feedback: Feedback shouldn't be overly positive — we avoid fake or doctored results
  • Supportive Environment: We should never make people feel bad for not being "S-Tier" — we all have off days and room to learn
  • Continuous Learning: We maintain consistency which is one of the pillars of quality

Ready to Start

When preparing your application build, focus on demonstrating these key qualities. Remember: we want more than just skilled builders — we're looking for teammates.

Show Your Best Work

  • Choose a project that showcases your strengths
  • Complete the build to a high standard — no half-finished work
  • Include both exterior and interior work
  • Demonstrate understanding of Star Trek design principles
  • Show your ability to work at scale and with complex proportions

Use Our Resources

  • Download our modpack to access custom blocks and textures
  • Study our existing builds for style guidance
  • Reference Star Trek canon for design inspiration
  • Practice with our building standards and proportions
Tell Your Story
  • Explain your design choices and creative process
  • Share what excites you about TrekCraft
  • Describe how you approach collaboration and feedback
  • Show your passion for Star Trek and building
  • Demonstrate your willingness to learn and grow
What We're Looking For
  • Technical Skill: Ability to work at scale with clean, precise construction
  • Creative Vision: Fresh ideas and problem-solving approaches
  • Team Player: Willingness to collaborate and share knowledge
  • Growth Mindset: Openness to feedback and continuous improvement
  • Passion: Genuine excitement for Star Trek and building
  • Commitment: Dedication to long-term project success
