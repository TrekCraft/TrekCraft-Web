Recruit Applications

What is a Recruit?

So you want to join TrekCraft? We're always welcoming new builders to join the team! New builders start out as an Recruit. A Recruit is given a small build yard to work on their own ship, paired with various builders to help them learn our tools, best practices, and to help them refine their build process.

Creating your Application

For your application we recommend doing a small build (A Runabout size shuttle, or the first deck of a ship). The idea is for us to get a view of your skillset, block pallet use, and ability to reference our build standards. Don't stress about a full-size ship for this though! We just need a small snapshot.

It is strongly recommend that you use the builds on server as reference for whatever you might be building.

Make sure to use our resource and mod pack for your application. We also recommend avoiding the use of vanilla textures when possible. While they are allowed, we want to see what you're able to do with our custom resources as we do not build with vanilla very often.

Submitting your Application

Once you're ready to apply, head to the #recruit-application page on our Discord. From there you can open your application, fill out our form, and attach pictures of your build. Make sure to review the steps carefully! Once our team reviews you should be given a decision and more details within about ~72hrs of applying.

Recruit Buildyard

The recurit buildyard is our area where recruits who pass our application process are able to build their own small-scale ship. This allows you to do a custom build alongside our build team, ask questions, and get feedback and training on how to execute your ideas as best as possible. You have the option of using one of our small hulls, bringing your own small build to the server, or building something entirely from scratch.

Recruit Buildyard Rules & FAQ
  • Players are not allowed to copy other players' work without permission.
  • The server builds can be used as examples to understand what makes a good project.
  • Players must stay within their assigned build plot.
  • For large-scale tasks like mirroring the ship, players can request World Edit assistance, but the rest of the building must be done by hand.
  • Players can ask staff members to alter their ship models, provided a staff member is willing to do so. However, continuous changes during the plot setup are discouraged to avoid excessive time consumption.
  • While players can ask for feedback and suggestions, staff members and other players cannot directly contribute to an individual player's Apprentice build. This is to assess the player's ability to work independently.
  • Players are required to plan their ships using the planning blocks provided in the mod pack. This helps them get accustomed to the standard planning and building practices used by the community.

    Can I have help with my application?

    While you're welcome to ask questions about the application process in our #sos-support channel, the application process is a solo one. If your application contains the work of others, it will be denied.

    Does it have to be Star Trek?

    No! You're welcome to submit whatever you like as long as it's relevant to TrekCraft. Try to keep it Sci-Fi if possible, and be mindful of the requirements listed above.

    How long do I have to complete my application?

    In terms of time, you will have as long as you need to complete your application to your satisfaction.

    What happens if I'm accepted/denied?

    If your application is denied, one of our staff will reach out to give you feedback on your application. We'd encourage you to use this feedback to make another attempt after 14-days. If you're accepted, you'll be welcomed into our private Recruit channel where we provide more details about who you'll be working with, and feedback on your work as you progress to full member.

    Does the form capture my personal information?

    No, and no one at TrekCraft will ever ask for your personal information for any reason. The note at the top of the form is placed their by Google as they track TOS violations of users submissions. This is not shared with us.
